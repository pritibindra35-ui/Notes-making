Main Notes
Making of Indian Constitution
  • Constituent Assembly: Formed in 1946, 389 members
  • Key Figures: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (Chairman, Drafting Committee), Nehru, Parel, Rajendra Prasad
  • Time taken: 2 years, 11 months, 18 days
  • Adopted: November 26, 1949; Enforced: January 26, 1950
Salient Features
  • Lengthy & Detailed: Originally 395 articles, 8 schedules
  • Federal with Unitary bias: Strong Center during emergencies
  • Parliamentary System: Borrowed from Britain
  • Fundamental Rights & Duties: Rights (Art. 12-35), Duties (Art. 51A)
  • Directive Principles: Non-justiciable guidelines for state policy
Summary

The Indian Constitution, crafted by the Constituent Assembly over nearly 3 years, represents a unique blend of features borrowed from various constitutions worldwide. It establishes India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic with a federal structure that can function unitarily during crises. The balance between rights and duties, along with directive principles, reflects the framers' vision of a just and equitable society.

Revision Strategies

The 3-2-1 Review Method

Review your notes at specific intervals:

  • 3 days after: First review - fill gaps, clarify doubts
  • 2 weeks after: Second review - create summaries
  • 1 month after: Final review - test yourself
This method uses spaced repetition to improve long-term retention.

Active Recall Techniques

  • Self-questioning: Turn headings into questions
  • Teach someone else: Explain concepts to friends/family
  • Flashcards: Key terms and definitions
  • Practice tests: Create mock questions
Active recall is more effective than passive re-reading.

Connection Mapping

Link different topics and concepts:

  • How does federalism relate to diversity?
  • Connection between rights and duties
  • Relationship between different institutions
  • Current affairs and theoretical concepts
Example: Constitution → Federalism → Center-State Relations → Current Issues like GST, Article 370
